Sustainability Through The Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time. A visual timeline of key milestones and targets toward a more sustainable future.

1968

Establishment

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore form a new company that would let them pursue innovation on their own terms.
1971

World's First

Intel introduces the world's first electronically programmable microprocessor, the 4004.
1978

10-Year Anniversary

Intel launches one landmark processor, the 8086 and initiates a new fellowship program to foster still more R&D
1979

Fame & Fortune

Intel makes its debut on the Fortune 500 list
1983

Comeback

Intel bounces back from the two-year recession by opening its largest fabrication facility up to that point, powering the landmark Compaq Portable computer and introducing new products that would lead revenues to exceed $1 billion for the first time in company history.
1986

Hardship Again

Intel faces the toughest economic year in its history as the semiconductor industry remains depressed. However, the company lays the groundwork for a more prosperous future, entering the market for ASICs and developing products with record-setting capabilities
1992

The Biggest

Intel is declared the biggest semiconductor manufacturer in the U.S., issued its first cash dividend to stockholders and increased its presence in retail outlets all in the same year.
1993

Any Volunteers?

Intel honored its 25th anniversary by launching its employee volunteer program, Intel Involved, which brought a new structure and permanence to employees’ efforts to help their communities
1995

Living on the Web

Intel launches its first website to help customers learn more about the company and its products.
2005

Another First

Intel releases its first multicore processor, dramatically advancing the computing power available in a single package.
2006

Brand New Look

Intel adopts a new logo — the company's biggest branding change since 1969 — signifying the company's commitment to pushing the envelope of what was possible from technology.
2011

Plus Ultra

Intel forms a new Mobile Communications Group, introduces the first generation of Thunderbolt technology and potentially saves the PC with the Ultrabook.
2016

Aerial Prowess

Intel's launches its first commercially available drone, the Falcon 8+, which offers easy remote access to areas humans would have trouble reaching.
2020

Pandemic Heroes

Intel releases the 11th generation Intel Core processor — and emphasizes ways its existing products could help the millions of people suddenly working and learning from home during the pandemic.
